\section{Exercise 590}

Given:
\[
    \left|
        \begin{array}{cc}
            \sin{\alpha}  & \cos{\alpha}\\
            -\cos{\alpha} & \sin{\alpha}\\
        \end{array}
    \right|
\]

Solution:
\[
    \left|
        \begin{array}{cc}
            \sin{\alpha}  & \cos{\alpha}\\
            -\cos{\alpha} & \sin{\alpha}\\
        \end{array}
    \right| = \sin{\alpha}\sin{\alpha} - \cos{\alpha}(-\cos{\alpha}) =
              \sin^{2}{\alpha} + \cos^{2}{\alpha} = 1
\]

Result:
\[
    \left|
        \begin{array}{cc}
            \sin{\alpha}  & \cos{\alpha}\\
            -\cos{\alpha} & \sin{\alpha}\\
        \end{array}
    \right| = 1
\]
