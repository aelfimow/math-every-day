\section{Exercise 860a}

Given:
\[
f(x) = \frac{\cos{x}}{1 - \sin{x}}
\]

Solution:
\[
f'(x) = \frac{(-\sin{x})(1 - \sin{x}) - \cos{x}(0 - \cos{x})}{(1 - \sin{x})^{2}} =
\]
\[
\frac{-\sin{x} + \sin^{2}{x} + \cos^{2}{x}}{(1 - \sin{x})^{2}} = \frac{-\sin{x} + 1}{(1 - \sin{x})^{2}} =
\]
\[
\frac{1 - \sin{x}}{(1 - \sin{x})^{2}} = \frac{1}{1 - \sin{x}}
\]

Result:
\[
f'(x) = \frac{1}{1 - \sin{x}}
\]
