\section{Exercise 956a}

Given:
\[
y = \euler^{-x}(\sin{x} + \cos{x})
\]

Solution:
\[
y' = -\euler^{-x}(\sin{x} + \cos{x}) + \euler^{-x}(\cos{x} - \sin{x}) =
\]
\[
\euler^{-x}(-\sin{x} - \cos{x} + \cos{x} - \sin{x}) = \euler^{-x}\cdot(-2\cdot\sin{x}) = -2\euler^{-x}\sin{x}
\]

Result:
\[
y' = -2\euler^{-x}\sin{x}
\]
